Along with Talaat and Djemal , he was one of the principal perpetrators of the Armenian Genocide[5][6][7 ] , of the Assyrian Genocide and of the Greek Genocide and thus is held responsible for the death of between 800,000 and 1,800,000[8][9][10][11 ] Armenians , 300,000 Assyrians and 350,000 Greeks . Prior to World War I , he was hailed at home as " the hero of the revolution " , and Europeans often spoke of Ottoman Turkey as " Enverland " .
