The convention is a successor to the International Agreement for the Regulation of Whaling , signed in London on 8 June 1937 , and the protocols for that agreement signed in London on 24 June 1938 , and 26 November 1945.The objectives of the agreement are the protection of all whale species from overhunting , the establishment of a system of international regulation for the whale fisheries to ensure proper conservation and development of whale stocks , and safeguarding for future generations the great natural resources represented by whale stocks .
