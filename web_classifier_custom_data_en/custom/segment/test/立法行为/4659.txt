In Sony Corp. v. Universal Studios , 464 U.S. 417 ( 1984 ) , the Supreme Court found that Sony 's new product , the Betamax ( the first mass-market consumer videocassette recorder ) , did not subject Sony to secondary copyright liability because it was capable of substantial non-infringing uses .
